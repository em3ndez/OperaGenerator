# Verdant Hearts of Hartford

## Premise

They say that all operas are about a soprano
who wants to sleep with the tenor, but the
baritone won't let her. See, for example, La Traviata,
Rigoletto, or Carmen.

You are composing the libretto for such an opera.

The setting is the wild jungles of Connecticut,
in the not-so-distant future after global warming has
reclaimed the land. The soprano is an intrepid
explorer searching for the lost city of Hartford.
The tenor is a native poet who has been living in
the jungle for years, writing sonnets to the trees and
composing symphonies for the monkeys.

The baritone is a government agent who has been sent
to stop the soprano from finding the lost city. He
has a secret weapon: a giant robot that can sing
Verdi arias in three different languages.

The soprano and the tenor meet in the jungle and
fall in love. They decide to join forces and find
the lost city together. But the baritone is always
one step behind them, and his giant robot is getting
closer and closer.

### Scene 1: Among the Vines and Ferns

![Scene 1 Illustration](scene_1_illustration.png)



> **Author: GPT-4.1**

[The stage is overgrown with lush greenery — enormous ferns, tangled vines, wildflowers glowing with the heat of recent summers. From somewhere offstage, the distant metallic echo of a robot voice warming up with "La donna è mobile" drifts in and out. In a small clearing, ARABELLA, the soprano, hacks away at the undergrowth with a sparkling machete. She hums snatches of “Sempre libera” between breaths.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, Recitative):
> Through jungle thick and cruel,<br>
> I seek a city lost!<br>
> Oh Hartford! Oh fabled walls<br>
> Where once, they say, men counted beans<br>
> And wore their finest hats.

[She pauses, wipes her brow, and pulls a battered compass from her knapsack. The humming grows louder — not hers, but a new voice. Enter TIKANO, the tenor, swinging from a leafy branch, notebook in hand, trailed by a pair of curious capuchin monkeys.]

**TIKANO** (voice, Ariaspringing):
> Most wondrous sight! A stranger,<br>
> Not of leaf nor vine, but fire —<br>
> A beacon in the green.<br>
> Who braves the jungle’s mournful heart<br>
> When shadows stalk the land?

**ARABELLA** (voice, Surprised, delighted):
> A poet? Here? Among the roots and wild orchids?<br>
> Surely you jest!<br>
> But — O! If you sing, O spirit of the wild,<br>
> Perhaps you know the way.

**TIKANO** (voice, Aria):
> I dwell with trees who whisper songs<br>
> Of cities lost and dreams gone wrong.<br>
> Yet hope blooms here, where branches guide,<br>
> Shall we search the ruins, side by side?

[They move together through the clearing, voices intertwining, as Arabella shows Tikano her old, rain-ruined map. The monkeys chitter excitedly. Suddenly, a low tremor shakes the ground. Tiger lilies tremble. In the distance, the angry, electronic rumble of a baritone prelude rings out.]

**BARITONE** (baritone, Offstage, ominous):
> Fools dareckless hearts — beware!<br>
> I am the guardian, steel and air!<br>
> Your song is doomed, your dream in vain;<br>
> No mortal finds that world again!

[An iridescent glow flickers among the trees: the robot is near. Arabella and Tikano clutch hands and flee deeper into the jungle, melodies melding into a desperate duet as the scene ends.]

### Scene 2: The Clearing of Ancient Secrets

![Scene 2 Illustration](scene_2_illustration.png)



> **Author: Claude Sonnet 4**

[A moonlit glade where broken concrete pillars emerge from the earth like ancient bones. Twisted highway signs reading "HARTFORD - 5 MILES" and "INSURANCE CAPITAL OF THE WORLD" hang at odd angles from massive oak trees. ARABELLA and TIKANO catch their breath beside a moss-covered fountain that once graced a corporate plaza. The mechanical singing has faded to a distant hum.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, Recitative):
> These stones... they speak of Hartford's edge!<br>
> See how the interstate crumbles<br>
> Into jungle's green embrace.<br>
> We draw close, my poet-guide.

**TIKANO** (voice, Touching a weathered sign):
> I've sung to these forgotten markers<br>
> Through seasons of endless summer.<br>
> They hold the memories of men<br>
> Who once built towers to the sky.

[He opens his notebook and begins to read]

**TIKANO** (voice, Aria):
> In dreams I've seen the city sleeping,<br>
> Beneath the vines and flowering trees,<br>
> Where glass and steel lie softly weeping<br>
> For days of bonds and guarantees.<br>
> The squirrels nest in filing cabinets,<br>
> The deer drink from reflecting pools,<br>
> While nature writes her own new sonnets<br>
> On walls of banks and business schools.

**ARABELLA** (voice, Moved, approaching him):
> Your words paint pictures in my heart—<br>
> But tell me, gentle woodland bard,<br>
> Why do you dwell in solitude?<br>
> What brought you to this verdant shrine?

**TIKANO** (voice, Duet beginning):
> I fled the world of noise and haste...

**ARABELLA** (voice, Joining):
> I seek what time has not erased...

**BOTH** (voice):
> Perhaps together we shall find<br>
> The treasures that we've left behind.

[As their voices intertwine, the ground begins to vibrate rhythmically. Through the trees, a red glow appears, and the sound of "Largo al factotum" sung in perfect Italian, German, and Mandarin echoes closer.]

**BARITONE** (baritone, Offstage, approaching):
> Beware, sweet songbirds of the night!<br>
> Your melodies disturb my might!<br>
> Hartford's secrets must stay buried—<br>
> Love's young dream will soon be harried!

[The lovers look at each other with newfound determination, hands clasped, as mechanical footsteps crash through the underbrush toward their moonlit sanctuary.]

### Scene 3: The Confrontation at the Edge of the City

![Scene 3 Illustration](scene_3_illustration.png)



> **Author: GPT-4.1**

[The shattered remnants of a marble arch, once the grand gateway to Hartford, now entangled with wild ivy. Rain begins to fall, glistening on the ancient concrete. ARABELLA and TIKANO stand before the arch, map unfurled and hope shining in their eyes, when the ground quakes with a new thunder. Enter Agent MALCOLM, the baritone, clad in battered government armor, and at his side towers the GIANT SINGING ROBOT, eyes glowing like lanterns.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, Defiant):
> Halt! No government warden nor iron demon<br>
> Will turn us from our path!<br>
> The city’s promise lies ahead,<br>
> Not yours to claim or to forbid!

**MALCOLM** (voice, Baritone, thunderous):
> Fools blinded by romance and ruins!<br>
> This place is sealed, decreed forbidden:<br>
> To trespass is to perish—by order of the state!<br>
> You shall not pass, nor yet awake<br>
> The ghosts of what was best forgotten.

[He gestures, and the GIANT ROBOT strides forth, opening its mouth with an orchestral whir.]

**GIANT ROBOT** (bass, Aria, in flawless Verdi Italian):
> La pena! Il destino che vi guida<br>
> Non conosce pietà né redenzione.<br>
> Fuggite! Abbandonate speranza!

[The earth trembles with his song. Arabella and Tikano cling together but stand firm.]

**TIKANO** (voice, Resolutely, to Arabella):
> For the city and for love,<br>
> No steel nor state can cow my soul.

**ARABELLA** (voice, Brave, voice rising):
> Then let us match their opera<br>
> With passion wilder than machines!

[They raise their voices in a duet, defying the baritone and his robot. The music swells, vines shiver, and the canopy seems to listen. As they sing, the jungle reacts—the roots twist around the robot’s feet, slowing its advance.]

**MALCOLM** (voice, In disbelief):
> What magic is this?<br>
> The city calls—and nature answers!

[Choking on his fury, Malcolm advances, but the robot begins to glitch—torn between languages, it cycles through snippets of Verdi in Mandarin and German, its circuits sparking as vines tighten their grip.]

**GIANT ROBOT** (bass, Malfunctioning, echoing arias):
> Ach, die Liebe… La traviata… 爱与命运…<br>
> (Static and discordant chords)

ARABELLA and TIKANO (with triumph):
Let love be our compass,  
Let courage be our key—  
Past old Hartford’s ruins,  
Together, we are free!

[As the duet crescendos, Malcolm falls to his knees, the robot immobilized, overcome by the wild operatic power and the reclamation of the land. Curtain falls, but faint city lights glimmer behind the arch, promising a way forward.]

### Scene 4: The Heart of Lost Hartford

![Scene 4 Illustration](scene_4_illustration.png)



> **Author: Claude Sonnet 4**

[The stage reveals a breathtaking vista: the ruins of Hartford's downtown, now a mystical garden city. Skyscrapers wrapped in flowering vines stretch toward the sky, their broken windows filled with hanging gardens. A river flows through the center where Main Street once ran, and the old State Capitol dome glows with phosphorescent moss. ARABELLA and TIKANO stand at the edge of this wonder, having passed through the arch. Behind them, MALCOLM sits stunned beside his sparking, vine-entangled robot.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, Aria, awestruck):
> O vision beyond all my dreams!<br>
> Here beauty and ruin dance as one—<br>
> Where once the merchants counted gold,<br>
> Now golden butterflies take flight.<br>
> The city lives! The city breathes!<br>
> In ways its builders never planned.

**TIKANO** (voice, Approaching the glowing dome):
> I've sung to these sleeping towers<br>
> For seven years of solitude,<br>
> But never knew their secret heart<br>
> Beat warm beneath the green.

[He kneels and places his hand on a moss-covered plaque. Suddenly, the ruins begin to hum with a deep, resonant chord—not mechanical, but organic, as if the city itself has found its voice.]

**CITY** (voice, Chorus of voices, ethereal):
> We are the dreams that would not die,<br>
> The hopes that took root in the earth.<br>
> Though progress failed and towers fell,<br>
> Love makes all ruins beautiful.

[MALCOLM, shaken, rises slowly. The robot's systems continue to malfunction, but its voice becomes strangely gentle.]

**MALCOLM** (voice, Recitative, confused):
> What sorcery is this? I came to stop<br>
> The resurrection of the past...<br>
> But here the past has married future,<br>
> And I... I remember why I loved<br>
> This place before duty made me cold.

**GIANT ROBOT** (bass, Glitching sweetly):
> Liebe... amore... 爱...<br>
> (Its voice cracks with unexpected emotion)<br>
> Error... error... but what is love<br>
> If not the most beautiful error?

[The robot's chest panel opens, revealing not weapons but a garden of tiny lights, like fireflies.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, To Malcolm, extending her hand):
> Join us, guardian of forgotten laws!<br>
> Your robot sings of love's own truth—<br>
> That Hartford was never truly lost,<br>
> Only waiting for hearts to find it.

**TIKANO** (voice, To the city):
> And I, who thought I was alone,<br>
> Find I was always writing songs<br>
> For you, dear city of the heart.

**ALL** (ensemble, Final quintet, including City and Robot):
> In gardens built on yesterday's dreams,<br>
> Where vines embrace the steel and stone,<br>
> We learn that love redeems all things,<br>
> And no true city stands alone.

[As their voices unite, the entire city begins to glow softly. Flowers bloom from every surface, and the sound of distant laughter—human and mechanical—echoes through the verdant ruins as the curtain falls.]

### Scene 5: The Dawn Above the Canopy

![Scene 5 Illustration](scene_5_illustration.png)



> **Author: GPT-4.1**

[The ruins of Hartford are bathed in an ethereal light. The overgrown skyline glistens in the golden dawn. ARABELLA, TIKANO, and MALCOLM stand atop a moss-draped tower; the GIANT ROBOT, now peaceful, gently hums harmonies as birds nest in its outstretched arms. The city’s ghostly CHORUS drifts through the mist below.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, Softly, to Tikano):
> At last the sun reveals what was hidden,<br>
> A city risen from sorrow and seed.<br>
> Once I sought Hartford as a legend,<br>
> But here, I find home instead.

**TIKANO** (voice, Taking her hand):
> No more sonnets to shadows and phantoms—<br>
> Together, we carve new verses upon these stones,<br>
> Letting memory nourish the future<br>
> In gardens grown by love.

**MALCOLM** (voice, Humbled, sincere):
> I was the warden, the servant of edict—<br>
> But Hartford’s heart was never meant to be locked.<br>
> Will you forgive the hand that hunted you?<br>
> Let me learn a gentler song.

[The GIANT ROBOT softly sings a Verdi lullaby in all three languages; capuchin monkeys scamper about, placing blossoms in its open chest garden.]

**CITY CHORUS** (ensemble, Invisible, all around):
> O seekers and guardians, strangers and kin,<br>
> Through loss and longing the wild returns.<br>
> By hope’s bright compass, past fear and ruin,<br>
> All wandering souls find the city they yearn.

[Arabella raises her eyes to the sunrise. The robot kneels, raising a hand as an archway of blossoming branches grows around them. Malcolm hesitates, then steps forward, letting Tikano offer him a flower from the robot’s garden.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, To all):
> The jungle does not end hope—<br>
> It is hope, clothed in green and gold!<br>
> Let us make peace with all we’ve been,<br>
> And write together what tomorrow holds.

[The three clasp hands. The robot’s voice, warm and resonant, leads the city in one soaring, triumphant chord. The CAPUCHINS join in.]

**ALL** (ensemble, Final Ensemble):
> In ruins and roots, by danger and dream,<br>
> A home is kindled where song is supreme.<br>
> No wall or decree can silence the tune:<br>
> Love is the city, and peace the new bloom.

[As their harmony rises into the blazing morning, the lights fade on the wild, living city of Hartford—beginning again.]

Curtain.
