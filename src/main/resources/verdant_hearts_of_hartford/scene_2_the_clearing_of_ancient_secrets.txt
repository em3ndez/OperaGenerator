Scene 2: The Clearing of Ancient Secrets
Author: Claude Sonnet 4

[A moonlit glade where broken concrete pillars emerge from the earth like ancient bones. Twisted highway signs reading "HARTFORD - 5 MILES" and "INSURANCE CAPITAL OF THE WORLD" hang at odd angles from massive oak trees. ARABELLA and TIKANO catch their breath beside a moss-covered fountain that once graced a corporate plaza. The mechanical singing has faded to a distant hum.]

**ARABELLA** (voice, Recitative):
> These stones... they speak of Hartford's edge!<br>
> See how the interstate crumbles<br>
> Into jungle's green embrace.<br>
> We draw close, my poet-guide.

**TIKANO** (voice, Touching a weathered sign):
> I've sung to these forgotten markers<br>
> Through seasons of endless summer.<br>
> They hold the memories of men<br>
> Who once built towers to the sky.

[He opens his notebook and begins to read]

**TIKANO** (voice, Aria):
> In dreams I've seen the city sleeping,<br>
> Beneath the vines and flowering trees,<br>
> Where glass and steel lie softly weeping<br>
> For days of bonds and guarantees.<br>
> The squirrels nest in filing cabinets,<br>
> The deer drink from reflecting pools,<br>
> While nature writes her own new sonnets<br>
> On walls of banks and business schools.

**ARABELLA** (voice, Moved, approaching him):
> Your words paint pictures in my heart—<br>
> But tell me, gentle woodland bard,<br>
> Why do you dwell in solitude?<br>
> What brought you to this verdant shrine?

**TIKANO** (voice, Duet beginning):
> I fled the world of noise and haste...

**ARABELLA** (voice, Joining):
> I seek what time has not erased...

**BOTH** (voice):
> Perhaps together we shall find<br>
> The treasures that we've left behind.

[As their voices intertwine, the ground begins to vibrate rhythmically. Through the trees, a red glow appears, and the sound of "Largo al factotum" sung in perfect Italian, German, and Mandarin echoes closer.]

**BARITONE** (baritone, Offstage, approaching):
> Beware, sweet songbirds of the night!<br>
> Your melodies disturb my might!<br>
> Hartford's secrets must stay buried—<br>
> Love's young dream will soon be harried!

[The lovers look at each other with newfound determination, hands clasped, as mechanical footsteps crash through the underbrush toward their moonlit sanctuary.]
